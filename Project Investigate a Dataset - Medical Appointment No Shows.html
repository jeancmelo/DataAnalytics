Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

Context: A person makes a doctor appointment, receives all the instructions and no-show. Who to blame?

Content: 110.527 medical appointments its 14 associated variables (characteristics).
The most important one if the patient show-up or no-show to the appointment.
Scholarship variable means this concept.
There are 14 variables.

Data Dictionary:

01 - PatientId Identification of a patient
02 - AppointmentID Identification of each appointment
03 - Gender Male or Female. Female is the greater proportion, woman takes way more care of their health in comparison to man.
04 - DataMarcacaoConsulta The day of the actual appointment, when they have to visit the doctor.
05 - DataAgendamento The day someone called or registered the appointment, this is before the appointment, of course.
06 - Age How old is the patient.
07 - Neighbourhood Where the appointment takes place.
08 - Scholarship True or False. Observation, this is a broad topic, consider reading this article Bolsa Família.
09 - Hipertension True or False.
10 - Diabetes True or False.
11 - Alcoholism True or False.
12 - Handicap True or False.
13 - SMS_received 1 or more messages sent to the patient.
14 - No-show True or False.

Impact of Time between Scheduling and Appointment¶

Question: Is the time gap between scheduling and the actual appointment related to the likelihood of a no-show?

In [111]:
# Converting date columns to datetime format
df['ScheduledDay'] = pd.to_datetime(df['ScheduledDay'])
df['AppointmentDay'] = pd.to_datetime(df['AppointmentDay'])

# Calculating the difference between the scheduling and appointment dates
df['Days_Difference'] = (df['AppointmentDay'] - df['ScheduledDay']).dt.days

# Creating time intervals for analysis
time_intervals = pd.cut(df['Days_Difference'], bins=[-1, 0, 1, 7, 30, 365], labels=['Same day', 'Next day', '1 week', '1 month', 'More than 1 month'])

# Adding the interval column to the DataFrame
df['Time_Interval'] = time_intervals

# Calculating the no-show rate by time interval
no_show_rate_by_time = df[df['No-show'] == 'Yes'].groupby('Time_Interval').size() / df.groupby('Time_Interval').size()

# Displaying the no-show rate by time interval
#print("No-show Rate by Time Interval:")
#print(no_show_rate_by_time)

# Plotting a bar chart for the no-show rate by time interval
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
plt.bar(no_show_rate_by_time.index, no_show_rate_by_time.values, color='#2020BA')

# Configuring title and axis labels
plt.title('No-show Rate by Time Interval')
plt.xlabel('Time Interval')
plt.ylabel('No-show Rate')

# Displaying the chart
plt.show()

Note:

It can be observed that the longer the interval between scheduling and the appointment, the higher the probability of a No-show.

Question: Are there recurring patterns of no-show associated with specific PatientIds, and is this related to specific health conditions?

# Identifying cases of non-attendance
no_show_cases = df[df['No-show'] == 'Yes']

# Checking for recurring PatientIds in non-attendance
recurring_patients_no_show = no_show_cases[no_show_cases.duplicated(subset=['PatientId'], keep=False)]

# Obtaining categories of health conditions with value 1
health_conditions = ['Hipertension', 'Diabetes', 'Alcoholism', 'Handcap']
health_conditions_with_value_1 = [condition for condition in health_conditions if recurring_patients_no_show[condition].any() == 1]

# Filtering only relevant columns of the DataFrame
df_filtered = recurring_patients_no_show[health_conditions_with_value_1]

# Counting the quantity of recurring patients in non-attendance for each category
results = df_filtered[health_conditions_with_value_1].sum()

# Creating a bar chart
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
results.plot(kind='bar', color=['#303090'])
plt.title('Recurring Patients in Non-Attendance by Health Condition (Value 1)')
plt.ylabel('Quantity of Recurring Patients')
plt.xlabel('Health Condition')
plt.show()

Conclusions¶

Based on the analyzed data, it is evident that the effectiveness of SMS reminders is significant, with a notable attendance rate for those who received messages. However, it is interesting to note that even without reminders, a substantial portion of patients attended appointments. Additionally, factors such as the interval between scheduling and the appointment, specific neighborhoods, and individual health conditions, especially hypertension, influence the no-show behavior. These trends highlight the complexity of the phenomenon and the need for a holistic approach to promote attendance.

Limitations and Considerations:

  • The analysis did not involve robust statistical tests and does not imply definitive conclusions.
  • Correlation between variables does not imply causality, and many unexplored factors may influence the results.
  • Due to the diversity of Brazil, a vast country, the lack of other specific data may limit more detailed analyses.

Next Steps:

  • A more in-depth investigation is recommended, considering unexplored variables such as socioeconomic data, for a comprehensive understanding.
  • Performing additional statistical analyses can provide more precise insights into the observed relationships.
